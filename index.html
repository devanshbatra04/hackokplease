ABOUT this EVENT

Hack OK Please is an intercollegiate Hackathon aimed at providing a platform for thinkers to ideate solutions around 'Build for India', thereby translating them into working prototypes in 24 hours of hacking period.

Product can be website, application, module, web extension, an improvement on existing applications, or simply anything that can be coded and sold as an achievement. Various technologies including but not limited to ML, AI, Blockchain, IoT can be incorporated into these themes.

Hack OK Please also offers an amazing opportunity to Interact with fellow developers, mentors and judges, thus building a timeless community of like-minded people!

Teams will be judged on innovation & ideation, feasibility of their product, proportion of the proposed product that they were able to build and the alignment of their solution with the theme.

Themes of this EVENT

We encourage invariably everyone - from newbies to frequent Hackathon goers to register for one of Delhi's biggest Hackathon where ideas on making for the country will acquire wings under the following tracks :

  1. Fitness - ‘Fit India’ is a movement to take the nation forward on the path of fitness and wellness. At Hack OK Please, we provide you a unique and exciting opportunity to work towards a healthier India.
  2. Waste Management - Swachh Bharat Abhiyan or Swachh Bharat Mission is a nation-wide campaign in India for the period 2014 to 2019 that aims to clean up the streets, roads and infrastructure of India's cities, towns, and rural areas. We urge you to brainstorm solutions that assist the movement
  3. Natural calamities - Beginning on 15 August 2018, Kerela saw the worst flooding in nearly a century. Drawing from that, we felt the need of building software solutions around Natural Calamities and Disasters.
  4. Tourism - Tourism in India is important for the country's economy and is growing rapidly. Be it designing applications for the existing industry or coming up with something to promote domestic tourism, we await your mind blowing ideas!
  5. Smart Mobility - Indians commute miles every single day. A significant fraction of our day is spent in travelling. Technology has played a very significant role in enabling more people to commute. Imagine how technology can make human experience even better in day to day transportation and commute. What can technology do make public and private transportation much simpler?

Rules of this EVENT

  1. Team size - upto 3 members
  2. Teams should be made up exclusively of students who are not organizers, volunteers, judges, sponsors, or in any other privileged position at the event.
  3. All team members should be present at the event for the entire duration of the hackathon
  4. Teams can of course gain advice and support from organizers, volunteers, sponsors, and others.
  5. All work on a project should be done at the hackathon. Teams will be subject to disqualification if found otherwise
  6. Teams can work only on ideas that align closely with the theme for the hack
  7. Teams can use libraries, frameworks, or open-source code in their projects.
  8. Working on a project before the event and open-sourcing it for the sole purpose of using the code during the event is against the spirit of the rules and is not allowed.
  9. Adding new features to existing projects is allowed. Judges will only consider new functionality introduced or new features added during the hackathon in determining the winners.
  10. Teams must stop hacking once the time is up. However, teams are allowed to debug and make small fixes to their programs after time is up. e.g. If during demoing your hack you find a bug that breaks your application and the fix is only a few lines of code, it's okay to fix that. Making large changes or adding new features is not allowed.
